In May 2015 I was approached by the McGill Rocket Team and asked to design the 10ft sticker that would wrap around the rocket's entire body. The sticker had to showcase all of the team's sponsors as well as the McGill Rocket Team's logo and 'Perigrine', the name of the rocket. This was McGill's first rocket ever built by McGill students so I was very excited to jump on board for this project and become part of the team.

The McGill Rocket Team builds experimental Sounding Rockets and participate in the Inter-Collegiate competition.

To design the sticker, I began by creating the pattern in Adobe Illustrator. Next I converted the file to a PSD file to open in Adobe Photoshop. From there, it was just a matter of putting the sponsor's logos, rocket name, and rocket logo ontop of the background pattern.

I ran into issues when some of the sponsor's logo files we're not in a vector based format, causing these images to become very pixelated. To solve this problem, I recreated the problematic logo's in Adobe Illustrator and saved them as PNG files - so that they can be resized to any dimension without loosing any quality.
